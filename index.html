About the Opera House

The Sydney Opera House is a multipurpose performing arts facility located on Port Jackson (Sydney Harbour), New South Wales, Australia. It is one of the most iconic and photographed buildings in the world due to its gleaming white sail-shaped shell roof structure. The building comprises multiple performance venues. The largest venue, the 2,679-seat Concert Hall, hosts many symphony concerts, choir performances, and music shows. The internationally acclaimed Sydney Symphony Orchestra performs more than 180 concerts a year with some of the world's most illustrious conductors and soloists. Whenever they perform at the Opera House, the temperature must be maintained at 72.5°F(22.5°C) in order to keep the musicians' instruments in tune. The heating and air-conditioning system is powered by cold seawater pulled directly from Sydney Harbour.

The opening of the Opera House was originally planned for Australian Day (January 26) in 1963, but cost overruns and structure engineering difficulties in executing the design troubled the course of the work, which forced many delays. After 14 years, it was finally completed on 20 October 1973 and formally opened by Queen Elizabeth II. The original cost was estimated at $7 million. The final cost was $102 million and it was largely paid by a State lottery. More than 10.9 million people visit the Opera House every year. Each year, Lunar New Year is celebrated at the Opera House with the sails lit in red, Lunar Lanterns and Mandarin tours. In 2019, some 250,000 people celebrated this.
